Three Different Dog Breeds

Breeds

Description

Siberian Husky

 A Siberian Husky is a dog breed with a very thick coat used for warmth against harsh and cold climates. They work in big groups like wolves, and they make perfect sled dogs due to their strength, speed, and size. Huskies are family friendly, and super loyal. Their unique traits include having neutral colored fur, and in some cases having heterochromia (Each eye having a different color).

Akita Inu

 Akita dogs are heavy, burly, and powerful spitz dogs based in Japan. "Akita" meaning any breed that has a bushy tail, erect ears, and thick coat, and "Inu" meaning dog in Japanese. They are a double-coated, muscular breed, which makes up their powerful trait. Akitas can come in colors like beige, and white. They are loyal to their owners, and very family friendly.

Finnish Spitz

 A Finnish Spitz is a small but brave breed. They almost take the appearance of that of a fox, with their flame-brown colored coat, and they hunting abilities. These spitz dogs are based in Finland, and are nicknamed "Finns" or "Finkies" by most. Their outstanding features include their shiny Finnish Spitz fur, their pointy ears, and curved tail.
